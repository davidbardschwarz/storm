COMMENT

Once you emerse yourself in the slow motion and the details of each of the people waiting, perhaps for a subway, there is the shocking appearance of a deluge of water. Freud describes such a moment of shock as follows: "(Shreck) is the name of the condition to which one is reduced if one encounters a danger without being prepared for it" (Freud 144).

CITATION

(Freud, Sigmund. Beyond the Pleasure Principle Translated by James Strachey. The Standard Edition (London: Hogard Press, 1920).
